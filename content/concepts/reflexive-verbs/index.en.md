# Reflexive Verbs (Verbos Reflexivos)



## What Are Reflexive Verbs?

**Reflexive verbs** describe actions that a subject performs **on or for themselves**. The infinitive ends in `-se`, and a reflexive pronoun (matching the subject) appears **before the conjugated verb**.

This construction is essential for daily routines, emotional states, and body-related actions.

### Core formula

```
reflexive pronoun + conjugated verb (-se)
```

---

## The 6 Reflexive Pronouns

| Person | Pronoun | Meaning | Example |
|:---:|:---:|:---|:---|
| 1st sg. | **me** | myself | *Yo me levanto.* (I get up.) |
| 2nd sg. | **te** | yourself (tú) | *Tú te duchas.* (You shower.) |
| 3rd sg. | **se** | him-/her-/yourself (Ud.) | *Él se viste.* (He gets dressed.) |
| 1st pl. | **nos** | ourselves | *Nosotros nos acostamos.* (We go to bed.) |
| 2nd pl. | **os** | yourselves (vosotros) | *Vosotros os laváis.* (You all wash up.) |
| 3rd pl. | **se** | themselves / you all (Uds.) | *Ellos se duermen.* (They fall asleep.) |

> **Note:** The 3rd-person singular and plural pronouns are both `se` — context clarifies who.

---

## Conjugation Patterns

Reflexive verbs follow the **same conjugation rules** as regular `-AR`, `-ER`, and `-IR` verbs. Only the pronoun changes.

### -AR pattern: **levantarse** (to get up)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | me levanto | I get up |
| tú | te levantas | you get up |
| él/ella/usted | se levanta | he/she gets up |
| nosotros/as | nos levantamos | we get up |
| vosotros/as | os levantáis | you all get up |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | se levantan | they get up |

### -ER pattern: **dormirse** (to fall asleep)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | me duermo | I fall asleep |
| tú | te duermes | you fall asleep |
| él/ella/usted | se duerme | he/she falls asleep |
| nosotros/as | nos dormimos | we fall asleep |
| vosotros/as | os dormís | you all fall asleep |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | se duermen | they fall asleep |

### -IR pattern: **vestirse** (to get dressed)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | me visto | I get dressed |
| tú | te vistes | you get dressed |
| él/ella/usted | se viste | he/she gets dressed |
| nosotros/as | nos vestimos | we get dressed |
| vosotros/as | os vestís | you all get dressed |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | se visten | they get dressed |

## Example Sentences

### Describing a daily routine

> *Por la mañana, me despierto a las 7.*
> (In the morning, I wake up at 7.)
>
> *Después, me ducho y me lavo los dientes.*
> (Then, I shower and brush my teeth.)
>
> *Me visto rápidamente y me voy al trabajo.*
> (I get dressed quickly and go to work.)
>
> *Por la noche, me acuesto a las 11.*
> (At night, I go to bed at 11.)

### Expressing emotions

> *¿Cómo te sientes hoy?*
> (How do you feel today?)
>
> *Me siento muy feliz.*
> (I feel very happy.)
>
> *Me preocupo por mi familia.*
> (I worry about my family.)

### Commands (Imperative)

> *¡Levántate!* — Get up! (tú)
> *¡Siéntate!* — Sit down! (tú)
> *¡Cállate!* — Shut up! (tú)

## Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

Korean has no grammatical reflexive concept, so these patterns tend to trip learners up:

1. **Word order — pronoun goes BEFORE the verb, not after.**
   - Korean: 나는 일어난다 → natural for Korean speakers to expect "Levanto me"
   - Spanish: Me levanto ✅

2. **Don't translate "-self" as a suffix.** English uses "wash oneself" — Spanish uses a separate pronoun: *Me lavo*.

3. **Pronoun can be dropped** when the subject is clear.
   - *Me levanto* = *Levanto* (informal, no ambiguity)

4. **Imperative placement differs.**
   - Affirmative command: pronoun attaches **after** the verb → *Levántate* (Get up)
   - Negative command: pronoun goes **before** the verb → *No te levantes* (Don't get up)

5. **Reflexive ≠ Passive.** Compare:
   - *Lavo el coche.* (I wash the car — regular verb, other object)
   - *Me lavo.* (I wash myself — reflexive)

## Advanced Usage Notes

### Emphatic / Adverbial Reflexives

Some reflexive verbs use the pronoun to add nuance, not strict self-action:

- *Me comí todo.* — I ate everything (emphatic completion)
- *Comí todo.* — I ate everything (neutral)
- *Me voy.* — I'm leaving (motivated departure)
- *Voy.* — I'm going (neutral motion)

### Reflexive + Emotion Adjective

`sentirse + adjective` is the standard pattern for stating feelings:

> *Me siento + cansado / feliz / triste / enojado / nervioso*
> (I feel tired / happy / sad / angry / nervous.)

### Reflexive + Preposition (Phrasal)

| Verb | Preposition | Example |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| **acostarse** *con* | with | *Me acuesto con él.* (I sleep with him.) |
| **despedirse** *de* | from | *Me despido de ti.* (I say goodbye to you.) |
| **quejarse** *de* | about | *Me quejo del ruido.* (I complain about the noise.) |
| **acordarse** *de* | of | *Me acuerdo de ti.* (I remember you.) |

## Practice Levels

### Level 1 — Conjugation Drill

Practice the 6 forms with 5 verbs:
- levantarse · ducharse · vestirse · acostarse · dormirse

### Level 2 — Sentence Building

Write 5–10 sentences describing your day, using only reflexive verbs.

### Level 3 — Conversational Prompts

- *¿A qué hora te levantas?* (What time do you get up?)
- *¿Dónde te duchas?* (Where do you shower?)
- *¿Con quién te acuestas?* (Who do you sleep with? — playful trick question)
