# Preterite Tense (Pretérito Indefinido)



## What Is the Preterite Tense?

Spanish has **two main past tenses**, and choosing between them is essential for fluent storytelling:

1. **Pretérito Indefinido** (this lesson) — completed actions in the past
2. **Pretérito Imperfecto** — ongoing or habitual past (see [[Imperfect_Tense_Spanish]])

**Pretérito Indefinido** describes actions that happened **once, at a specific moment**, and are now **finished**. It corresponds to English simple past: "I ate," "I did," "I went."

### When to use it

- Completed actions: *Ayer comí pizza.* (Yesterday I ate pizza.)
- Sequential events: *Entré, miré y salí.* (I went in, looked, and left.)
- Specific facts: *Colón descubrió América en 1492.* (Columbus discovered America in 1492.)
- Specific time: *A las 3, llamé.* (At 3, I called.)

## The Three Regular Conjugation Patterns

### `-AR` verbs — example: **hablar** (to speak)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | hablé | I spoke |
| tú | hablaste | you spoke |
| él/ella/usted | habló | he/she spoke |
| nosotros/as | hablamos | we spoke |
| vosotros/as | hablasteis | you all spoke |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | hablaron | they spoke |

**Ending pattern:** `-é, -aste, -ó, -amos, -asteis, -aron`

### `-ER` verbs — example: **comer** (to eat)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | comí | I ate |
| tú | comiste | you ate |
| él/ella/usted | comió | he/she ate |
| nosotros/as | comimos | we ate |
| vosotros/as | comisteis | you all ate |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | comieron | they ate |

**Ending pattern:** `-í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -isteis, -ieron`

### `-IR` verbs — example: **vivir** (to live)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | viví | I lived |
| tú | viviste | you lived |
| él/ella/usted | vivió | he/she lived |
| nosotros/as | vivimos | we lived |
| vosotros/as | vivisteis | you all lived |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | vivieron | they lived |

**Ending pattern:** `-í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -isteis, -ieron`

> **Note:** `-ER` and `-IR` preterite endings are **almost identical** — they only differ in the 3rd person plural (`-ieron` vs `-ieron` — actually the same). Treat them as one group.

## Essential Irregular Verbs (Preterite)

These high-frequency verbs have irregular preterite forms. Memorize them early.

### Stem-changing + irregular endings

| Spanish | yo | tú | él | nosotros | ellos |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **tener** | tuve | tuviste | tuvo | tuvimos | tuvieron |
| **estar** | estuve | estuviste | estuvo | estuvimos | estuvieron |
| **andar** | anduve | anduviste | anduvo | anduvimos | anduvieron |
| **poder** | pude | pudiste | pudo | pudimos | pudieron |
| **poner** | puse | pusiste | puso | pusimos | pusieron |
| **saber** | supe | supiste | supo | supimos | supieron |
| **caber** | cupe | cupiste | cupo | cupimos | cupieron |
| **haber** | hube | hubiste | hubo | hubimos | hubieron |
| **hacer** | hice | hiciste | hizo | hicimos | hicieron |
| **querer** | quise | quisiste | quiso | quisimos | quisieron |
| **venir** | vine | viniste | vino | vinimos | vinieron |
| **decir** | dije | dijiste | dijo | dijimos | dijeron |
| **traer** | traje | trajiste | trajo | trajimos | trajeron |
| **conducir** | conduje | condujiste | condujo | condujimos | condujeron |

### Fully irregular (yo-form + 3rd persons)

| Spanish | yo | tú | él | nosotros | ellos |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **ser / ir** (shared!) | fui | fuiste | fue | fuimos | fueron |
| **dar** | di | diste | dio | dimos | dieron |
| **ver** | vi | viste | vio | vimos | vieron |

> **Special:** **ser** and **ir** share identical preterite forms. Context disambiguates:
> *Fui médico* (I was a doctor) vs *Fui al médico* (I went to the doctor).

## Preterite vs. Imperfecto — Quick Decision Guide

| If the past action... | Use |
|:---|:---|
| Happened once and ended | **Pretérito Indefinido** |
| Was in progress / was happening | Pretérito Imperfecto |
| Was habitual / repeated | Pretérito Imperfecto |
| Sets the scene (background) | Pretérito Imperfecto |
| Is the main event of the story | **Pretérito Indefinido** |
| Interrupted an ongoing action | **Pretérito Indefinido** |

### Common pattern

> *Yo **leía** (background — Imperfecto) cuando **llegó** (event — Indefinido) María.*
> (I was reading when María arrived.)

## Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

1. **Korean past tense is uniform ("~했다").** Spanish requires a distinct ending for each of the 6 persons — memorization required.
2. **-ER and -IR are practically the same in preterite.** Don't waste time separating them.
3. **Don't confuse "hablé" with "hablaba."** *Hablé* = I spoke (one event). *Hablaba* = I was speaking / used to speak (ongoing).
4. **Irregular verbs in preterite are NOT like present-tense irregulars.** The same verb may have very different irregularities across tenses.
5. **Time expressions are essential context.** Without *ayer* or *la semana pasada*, the listener may assume you're using imperfecto.

## Example Sentences

### Single completed events

> *Ayer **comí** pizza.* (Yesterday I ate pizza.)
> *Esta mañana **desayuné** café.* (This morning I had coffee for breakfast.)
> *El año pasado **viajé** a España.* (Last year I traveled to Spain.)

### Sequential events

> ***Entré**, **miré** y **salí**.* (I went in, looked, and left.)
> ***Llegué** a casa, **cociné** y **dormí**.* (I got home, cooked, and slept.)

### Specific facts

> *Colón **descubrió** América en 1492.* (Columbus discovered America in 1492.)
> *Einstein **publicó** su teoría en 1905.* (Einstein published his theory in 1905.)

### With time expressions

> *Hace dos semanas **compré** un coche.* (Two weeks ago I bought a car.)
> *En 2020 **empecé** a estudiar español.* (In 2020 I started studying Spanish.)

## Practice Levels

### Level 1 — Conjugation drill

Practice the 6 forms with 5 regular verbs across all 3 groups.

### Level 2 — Sentence building

Write 5–7 sentences about yesterday using **preterite** and a time adverb.

### Level 3 — Irregular mastery

Memorize the 14 most common irregular preterite verbs and use them in 10 conversational sentences.

### Level 4 — Mixed past

Combine preterite with imperfecto: *Estudiaba cuando me llamaste.* (I was studying when you called me.)
