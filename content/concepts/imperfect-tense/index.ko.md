## 📌 한국어 학습자를 위한 요약

- **핵심:** 불완료 과거 시제. 과거에 **진행 중이던** 동작, **반복되던 습관**, **배경 묘사** 에 사용. 영어 "I was eating / I used to eat / I would eat" 에 해당.
- **vs 완료 과거:** Pretérito Indefinido (끝남) ↔ Pretérito Imperfecto (진행/반복). 비교는 [[Past_Tense_Spanish]] 참조.
- **3그룹 동사 변화:** `-AR` (hablar) / `-ER` (comer) / `-IR` (vivir) — `-ER`와 `-IR` 변형 동일
- **불규칙 동사 3개:** ser / ir / ver — 거의 외우기만 하면 됨
- **활용:** 옛날 이야기, 습관 묘사, 시간/장소 배경
- **한국어 학습자 주의:** "어제 먹었다" (Indefinido, comí) vs "예전엔 매일 먹었다" (Imperfecto, comía) — 한국어로는 구분이 미묘

# Imperfect Tense (Pretérito Imperfecto)



## What Is the Imperfect Tense?

**Pretérito Imperfecto** describes past actions that were **ongoing**, **habitual**, or provided **background description**. It corresponds to English:

- "I was eating" (in progress)
- "I used to eat" (habitual)
- "I would eat" (repeated in the past)

It does **not** describe a completed event — that's [[Past_Tense_Spanish|Pretérito Indefinido]].

### When to use it

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| Past action in progress | *Yo leía cuando llegó María.* (I was reading when María arrived.) |
| Habitual past action | *De niño, jugaba al fútbol.* (As a child, I used to play soccer.) |
| Background description | *Era las tres. Hacía sol.* (It was 3 o'clock. It was sunny.) |
| Time / date / age | *Eran las cinco. Teníamos veinte años.* |
| Mental / emotional state | *Quería descansar.* (I wanted to rest.) |

## The Three Regular Conjugation Patterns

### `-AR` verbs — example: **hablar** (to speak)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | hablaba | I used to speak |
| tú | hablabas | you used to speak |
| él/ella/usted | hablaba | he/she used to speak |
| nosotros/as | hablábamos | we used to speak |
| vosotros/as | hablabais | you all used to speak |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | hablaban | they used to speak |

**Ending pattern:** `-aba, -abas, -aba, -ábamos, -abais, -aban`

> **Note:** 1st and 3rd person singular are identical (`-aba`). Context clarifies.

### `-ER` verbs — example: **comer** (to eat)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | comía | I used to eat |
| tú | comías | you used to eat |
| él/ella/usted | comía | he/she used to eat |
| nosotros/as | comíamos | we used to eat |
| vosotros/as | comíais | you all used to eat |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | comían | they used to eat |

**Ending pattern:** `-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían`

### `-IR` verbs — example: **vivir** (to live)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | vivía | I used to live |
| tú | vivías | you used to live |
| él/ella/usted | vivía | he/she used to live |
| nosotros/as | vivíamos | we used to live |
| vosotros/as | vivíais | you all used to live |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | vivían | they used to live |

**Ending pattern:** `-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían`

> **Note:** `-ER` and `-IR` imperfect endings are **completely identical**. Only `-AR` verbs use `-aba-`; the other two use `-ía-`.

## Ending Comparison Table

| Person | `-AR` | `-ER` | `-IR` |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| yo | habl**aba** | com**ía** | viv**ía** |
| tú | habl**abas** | com**ías** | viv**ías** |
| él/ella/ud. | habl**aba** | com**ía** | viv**ía** |
| nosotros | habl**ábamos** | com**íamos** | viv**íamos** |
| vosotros | habl**abais** | com**íais** | viv**íais** |
| ellos/uds. | habl**aban** | com**ían** | viv**ían** |

> **Korean speaker tip:** Indefinido and Imperfecto have **different endings**, so you can always tell them apart from the verb form alone. The 1st and 3rd person singular of `-AR` Imperfecto (`hablaba`) is one notable exception — context clarifies.

## The Only 3 Irregular Verbs

The imperfect tense has **only 3 irregular verbs** in the entire language. Everything else is regular.

| Spanish | yo | él/ella | nosotros | ellos |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **ser** (to be, essence) | era | era | éramos | eran |
| **ir** (to go) | iba | iba | íbamos | iban |
| **ver** (to see) | veía | veía | veíamos | veían |

> **Note:** *ser* and *ir* share identical 1st and 3rd person singular forms (`era`, `iba`). Context is required to distinguish them.

### Examples of irregular use

> ***Era** estudiante cuando tenía veinte años.* (I was a student when I was 20 years old — **ser**.)
> ***Iba** al colegio en autobús.* (I used to go to school by bus — **ir**.)
> *De niño, **veía** mucha televisión.* (As a child, I used to watch a lot of TV — **ver**.)

These adverbs and phrases typically signal imperfecto usage:

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|

| **de niño / de niña** | as a child |

## Indefinido vs. Imperfecto — Decision Tree

```
1. Did the past action end at a specific moment?
   → Yes: Use PRETÉRITO INDEFINIDO
   → No: Continue

2. Was the past action in progress / repeated?
   → Yes: Use PRETÉRITO IMPERFECTO
   → No: Continue

3. Is it background / description (time, place, age)?
   → Yes: Use PRETÉRITO IMPERFECTO
```

### Quick pattern

> *Yo **leía** (background — Imperfecto) cuando **llegó** (event — Indefinido) María.*
> *Hacía (background) sol cuando entré (event) en la sala.*

### Common combinations

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Imperfecto + **cuando** + Indefinido | was doing X when Y happened | *Dormía cuando me llamaste.* |
| Imperfecto + Imperfecto | two simultaneous ongoing actions | *Leía y escuchaba música.* |
| Imperfecto + Indefinido | background + main event | *Era tarde y llovía.* |
| **Siempre** + Imperfecto | always used to | *Siempre llegaba tarde.* |

## Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

1. **Korean past tense doesn't distinguish completed vs. ongoing.** Spanish requires choosing between Indefinido and Imperfecto — this is the hardest part of past-tense mastery.
2. **Don't translate "used to" literally.** Spanish uses Imperfecto, not a separate verb.
3. **"Era" can be confusing** — it's both *ser* (was) and could be confused with *estar* (estaba). They differ by meaning.
4. **Don't overuse Imperfecto for simple past facts.** *Viví en Madrid* (I lived in Madrid once) vs *Vivía en Madrid* (I used to live in Madrid).
5. **Don't confuse Imperfecto with present progressive.** *Leía* (I was reading) ≠ *Estoy leyendo* (I am reading).

## Example Sentences

### Habitual past

> *Cuando **era** niño, **vivía** en Seúl.* (When I was a child, I lived in Seoul.)
> *Mi madre siempre **cocinaba** paella los domingos.* (My mother always used to cook paella on Sundays.)
> *De joven, **jugaba** al fútbol todos los días.* (As a young person, I used to play soccer every day.)

### Background description

> ***Hacía** frío y **nevaba**.* (It was cold and snowing.)
> ***Eran** las tres de la tarde y todo el mundo **dormía**.* (It was 3 PM and everyone was sleeping.)

### Interrupted action

> *Yo **leía** un libro cuando **llegó** María.* (I was reading a book when María arrived.)
> ***Estudiábamos** español cuando **empezó** a llover.* (We were studying Spanish when it started to rain.)

### Mental / emotional state

> *Antes no **había** tantos restaurantes coreanos.* (Before, there weren't so many Korean restaurants.)
> *¿Qué **hacías** cuando te **llamé**?* (What were you doing when I called you?)
> ***Quería** viajar a Argentina, pero no **tenía** dinero.* (I wanted to travel to Argentina, but I didn't have money.)

### Mixed past (Imperfecto + Indefinido)

> ***Era** tarde cuando **volví** a casa.* (It was late when I returned home.)
> ***Tenía** veinte años cuando **empecé** la universidad.* (I was twenty when I started university.)

## Practice Levels

### Level 1 — Conjugation drill

Practice the 6 forms with 5 regular verbs in all 3 groups.

### Level 2 — Habitual past narrative

Write 5–7 sentences about what you **used to do** as a child, using time adverbs (de niño, siempre, a veces).

### Level 3 — Background + event

Write 10 sentences combining Imperfecto (background) and Indefinido (event) using **cuando**.

### Level 4 — Irregular mastery

Use *ser*, *ir*, *ver* in Imperfecto in 10 conversational sentences.
