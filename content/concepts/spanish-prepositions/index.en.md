# Spanish Prepositions (Preposiciones)



## What Are Prepositions?

Prepositions connect nouns, pronouns, or phrases to other words in a sentence, expressing relationships like location, direction, time, possession, and purpose. Spanish has a smaller set of core prepositions than English, but each has multiple uses.

---

## The 4 Essential Prepositions (90% of usage)

### **a** — direction, destination, indirect object marker

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| Direction / destination | *Voy **a** Madrid.* (I'm going to Madrid.) |
| Indirect object | *Le doy el libro **a** María.* (I give the book to María.) |
| Time (at) | **A** las tres.* (At three o'clock.) |
| Verb + a + infinitive (future) | *Voy **a** comer.* (I'm going to eat.) |

### **de** — origin, possession, material

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| Origin / from | *Soy **de** Corea.* (I'm from Korea.) |
| Possession | *El libro **de** Juan.* (Juan's book.) |
| Material | *Una mesa **de** madera.* (A wooden table.) |
| Content / contents | *Una taza **de** café.* (A cup of coffee.) |

### **en** — location, transport, time

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| Location (in/at/on) | *Estoy **en** casa.* (I'm at home.) |
| Transportation | *Viajo **en** tren.* (I travel by train.) |
| Time (in/at) | ***En** enero hace frío.* (In January it's cold.) |
| Manner (in) | *Lo digo **en** español.* (I say it in Spanish.) |

### **con** — accompaniment, instrument, manner

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| With (companion) | *Voy **con** María.* (I'm going with María.) |
| With (instrument) | *Escribo **con** un bolígrafo.* (I write with a pen.) |
| Manner | *Habla **con** cuidado.* (Speak with care.) |

## por vs. para — The Classic Confusion

This is the most challenging pair for Spanish learners. Both translate roughly to "for" in English but have distinct uses.

### **por** — cause, passage, exchange, duration

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| Reason / cause | *Gracias **por** todo.* (Thanks for everything.) |
| Through / along | *Paseo **por** el parque.* (I walk through the park.) |
| Exchange (in exchange for) | *Pago 10 euros **por** el libro.* (I pay 10 euros for the book.) |
| Duration (in/for) | *Estudio **por** dos horas.* (I study for two hours.) |
| On behalf of | *Trabajo **por** mi hermano.* (I work on behalf of my brother.) |
| Means of transport | *Viajo **por** avión.* (I travel by plane.) |
| Movement around | *Camino **por** la calle.* (I walk along the street.) |

### **para** — purpose, destination, deadline, recipient

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| Purpose / goal | *Estudio **para** aprender.* (I study in order to learn.) |
| Recipient | *Este regalo es **para** ti.* (This gift is for you.) |
| Destination (person) | *Salgo **para** Madrid mañana.* (I'm leaving for Madrid tomorrow.) |
| Deadline / by | ***Para** el lunes, por favor.* (By Monday, please.) |
| Comparison | *Para ser niño, lee muy bien.* (For a child, he reads very well.) |
| Employment | *Trabajo **para** Google.* (I work for Google.) |

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| Context | por | para |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Working | *Trabajo **por** la noche.* (I work **at** night.) | *Trabajo **para** Google.* (I work **for** Google.) |
| Time | *Estudio **por** 2 horas.* (I study **for** 2 hours.) | *Lo necesito **para** mañana.* (I need it **by** tomorrow.) |
| Travel | *Paseo **por** el parque.* (I walk **through** the park.) | *Salgo **para** el parque.* (I'm leaving **for/to** the park.) |
| Reason/Purpose | *Lo hice **por** ti.* (I did it **because of** you.) | *Lo hice **para** ti.* (I did it **for** you.) |

> **Memory aid:** **Por** is for the *journey* (through, along, by, because of). **Para** is for the *destination* (in order to, for the sake of, by deadline).

| Spanish | English | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **a la izquierda** | to the left | *Gire a la izquierda.* |
| **a la derecha** | to the right | *Gire a la derecha.* |

| **entre** | between | *Entre la plaza y el museo.* |

| **cerca de** | near | *Cerca de aquí.* |
| **lejos de** | far from | *Lejos de la ciudad.* |

## Time Prepositions

| Spanish | English | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **a las** | at (clock time) | *A las tres.* |
| **antes de** | before | *Antes de comer.* |
| **después de** | after | *Después del trabajo.* |
| **desde** | since | *Desde 2020.* |
| **hasta** | until | *Hasta las cinco.* |
| **en** | in (month/year/season) | *En enero / En 2026 / En verano.* |
| **durante** | during | *Durante el día.* |
| **entre** | between (range) | *Entre las 2 y las 4.* |

## Contractions: a + el = al, de + el = del

Only `a` and `de` contract with the masculine singular article **el**.

| Article | a + el | de + el |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Singular | **al** | **del** |
| Plural (a los / de los) | a los | de los |
| Feminine (a la / de la) | a la | de la |

### Examples

> *Voy **al** supermercado.* (I'm going **to the** supermarket.)
> *Vengo **del** trabajo.* (I'm coming **from** work.)
> *El libro **de la** profesora.* (The teacher's book — no contraction needed with feminine.)

---

## Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

1. **Korean particles are flexible; Spanish prepositions are strict.** You can't freely drop a Spanish preposition the way you might drop a Korean particle.
2. **Don't over-rely on direct translation.** Korean ~에서 maps to Spanish en, de, or desde depending on nuance.
3. **por vs para requires context.** Memorize example phrases, not abstract rules.
4. **Don't forget "a" as the indirect object marker.**
   - *Le doy el libro **a** María.* — required, not optional
5. **"al" and "del" are obligatory contractions.** Always write them as one word.

## Example Sentences

### Basic usage

> *Voy **a** la escuela.* (I go to school.)
> *Vengo **de** casa.* (I come from home.)
> *Estoy **en** el parque.* (I'm in the park.)
> *Hablo **con** mi amigo.* (I talk with my friend.)

### Direction and location

> *El hotel está **a** la izquierda del museo.* (The hotel is to the left of the museum.)
> *El banco está **entre** la farmacia y el restaurante.* (The bank is between the pharmacy and the restaurant.)
> *Vivo **cerca de** la estación.* (I live near the station.)

### por vs para

> *Estudio español **por** trabajo.* (I study Spanish for work — cause.)
> *Estudio español **para** viajar.* (I study Spanish to travel — purpose.)
> *Paseo **por** el centro.* (I walk through downtown.)
> *Salgo **para** el centro.* (I'm leaving for downtown.)
