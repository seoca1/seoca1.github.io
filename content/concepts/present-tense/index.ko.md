# Present Tense (Presente)



## What Is the Spanish Present Tense?

The **Presente** describes actions happening now, habitual actions, general truths, and current states. It corresponds to English "I speak," "I eat," "I live" — covering both "I am speaking" (progressive) and "I speak [every day]" (habitual).

In Spanish, every verb's infinitive ends in `-AR`, `-ER`, or `-IR`, and each group has a distinct conjugation pattern across the 6 persons.

---

## The Three Regular Conjugation Patterns

### `-AR` verbs — example: **hablar** (to speak)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | hablo | I speak |
| tú | hablas | you speak |
| él/ella/usted | habla | he/she speaks |
| nosotros/as | hablamos | we speak |
| vosotros/as | habláis | you all speak |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | hablan | they speak |

**Ending pattern:** `-o, -as, -a, -amos, -áis, -an`

### `-ER` verbs — example: **comer** (to eat)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | como | I eat |
| tú | comes | you eat |
| él/ella/usted | come | he/she eats |
| nosotros/as | comemos | we eat |
| vosotros/as | coméis | you all eat |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | comen | they eat |

**Ending pattern:** `-o, -es, -e, -emos, -éis, -en`

### `-IR` verbs — example: **vivir** (to live)

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| yo | vivo | I live |
| tú | vives | you live |
| él/ella/usted | vive | he/she lives |
| nosotros/as | vivimos | we live |
| vosotros/as | vivís | you all live |
| ellos/ellas/ustedes | viven | they live |

**Ending pattern:** `-o, -es, -e, -imos, -ís, -en`

> **Note:** `-ER` and `-IR` differ only in the **nosotros** and **vosotros** forms. Otherwise identical.

## Essential Irregular Verbs

Some high-frequency verbs don't follow the regular patterns. Memorize these first.

### "Yo-go" verbs (irregular only in the yo form)

| Infinitive | yo form | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **tener** | tengo | to have |
| **venir** | vengo | to come |
| **hacer** | hago | to do / make |
| **poner** | pongo | to put |
| **salir** | salgo | to leave |
| **traer** | traigo | to bring |
| **decir** | digo | to say |
| **oír** | oigo | to hear |

The other 5 persons follow regular `-ER`/`-IR` patterns.

### Spelling-change verbs (e → ie, o → ue)

Some verbs change the stem vowel in stressed syllables:

| Infinitive | yo | tú | él | nosotros | ellos |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **pensar** (to think) | pienso | piensas | piensa | pensamos | piensan |
| **volver** (to return) | vuelvo | vuelves | vuelve | volvemos | vuelven |
| **dormir** (to sleep) | duermo | duermes | duerme | dormimos | duermen |
| **poder** (to be able) | puedo | puedes | puede | podemos | pueden |
| **querer** (to want) | quiero | quieres | quiere | queremos | quieren |

> **Yo and nosotros** typically keep the original vowel (pensamos, podemos).

### Fully irregular — top 5

| Spanish | English | yo | tú | él | nosotros | ellos |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **ser** | to be (essence) | soy | eres | es | somos | son |
| **estar** | to be (state) | estoy | estás | está | estamos | están |
| **ir** | to go | voy | vas | va | vamos | van |
| **tener** | to have | tengo | tienes | tiene | tenemos | tienen |
| **hacer** | to do/make | hago | haces | hace | hacemos | hacen |

## Common Uses of the Present Tense

| Use | Example |
|:---|:---|
| **Current action** | *Ahora hablo español.* (I'm speaking Spanish now.) |
| **Habitual action** | *Desayuno a las 7.* (I have breakfast at 7.) |
| **General truth** | *El sol sale por el este.* (The sun rises in the east.) |
| **Scheduled event** | *El tren sale a las 9.* (The train leaves at 9.) |
| **Ongoing story (narrative)** | *Y entonces entra el villano...* (And then the villain enters...) |

---

## Common Mistakes for Korean Speakers

1. **Korean has no verb conjugation for person.** Spanish requires distinct endings for each person — there's no way around learning them.
2. **Subject pronouns are often dropped.** Since the verb ending encodes the subject, *Hablo español* = *Yo hablo español*.
3. **Don't confuse ser / estar** — both mean "to be" but with different nuance (essence vs state).
4. **Don't confuse -ER and -IR nosotros.** Memorize: `comemos` (e) but `vivimos` (i).
5. **"Going to + verb" = ir + a + infinitive, not a tense.**
   - *Voy a hablar* (I'm going to speak) — present of *ir* + *a* + infinitive

## Example Sentences

### Routine (habitual present)

> *Me levanto a las siete, desayuno café y voy al trabajo.*
> (I get up at 7, have coffee for breakfast, and go to work.)

### Description (current state)

> *Mi hermana vive en Madrid y trabaja en una escuela.*
> (My sister lives in Madrid and works at a school.)

### General truth

> *En Seúl, el invierno es frío pero el verano es caluroso.*
> (In Seoul, winter is cold but summer is hot.)

### Scheduled event

> *El avión sale a las tres de la tarde.*
> (The plane leaves at 3 PM.)
