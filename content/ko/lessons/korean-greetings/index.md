> 1. 가장 기본 인사: 안녕하세요 (만남), 안녕히 가세요/계세요 (작별), 잘 가/있어 (비격식)

# Korean Greetings & Self-Introduction

## 1. Hello, Goodbye

| Korean | Pronunciation | When | Formality |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **안녕하세요?** | annyeong-haseyo | Hello (anytime) | Formal |
| **안녕** | annyeong | Hi (friends, young) | Casual |
| **안녕히 가세요** | annyeong-hi gaseyo | Goodbye (you leaving) | Formal |
| **안녕히 계세요** | annyeong-hi gyeseyo | Goodbye (I'm leaving) | Formal |
| **잘 가** | jal ga | Bye (leaving) | Casual |
| **잘 있어** | jal isseo | Bye (staying) | Casual |

> 💡 **안녕히 가세요** = "Go in peace" (you, the leaver). **안녕히 계세요** = "Stay in peace" (you, the stayer).

## 2. Time-of-Day Greetings

| Korean | Time | English |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **좋은 아침이에요** | morning | Good morning |
| **좋은 오후에요** | afternoon | Good afternoon |
| **좋은 저녁이에요** | evening | Good evening |

## 3. How Are You?

| Korean | Formality | Use |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| **잘 지내세요?** | Formal | How are you (honorific)? |
| **잘 지내?** | Casual | How are you (informal)? |
| **어때요?** | Neutral | How's it going? |
| **별일 없죠?** | Neutral | Nothing much, right? |

Responses:
- **잘 지내요** = I'm doing well
- **네, 잘 지내요** = Yes, I'm well
- **그저 그래요** = So-so
- **별로예요** = Not great

## 4. Self-Introduction Pattern

The classic structure:

```
이름 + (입니다/이에요)
      + 나이/국적/직업
      + 취미/관심사
```

### Formal (business, strangers, older people)

| Korean | Pronunciation | English |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **안녕하세요? 제 이름은 마크입니다.** | annyeong-haseyo, je ireumeun Makeu-imnida | Hello, my name is Mark. |
| **저는 28살입니다.** | jeoneun 28-sal-imnida | I'm 28 years old. |
| **미국에서 왔습니다.** | migug-eseo wasseumnida | I'm from America. |
| **회사원입니다.** | hoesawon-imnida | I'm an office worker. |
| **만나서 반갑습니다.** | mannaseo bangapseumnida | Nice to meet you. |

### Casual (friends, peers)

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **안녕! 나는 마크야.** | Hi, I'm Mark. |
| **28살이야.** | I'm 28. |
| **미국에서 왔어.** | I'm from the US. |
| **대학생이야.** | I'm a college student. |
| **만나서 반가워.** | Nice to meet you. |

> Note: **저 (jeo)** = formal "I", **나 (na)** = casual "I". Same with **저는/나는**.

## 5. Honorifics (높임말)

Korean has **6 speech levels**. The most common:

| Ending | Formality | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **~습니다/ㅂ니다** | Very formal (news, speeches) | **합니다** |
| **~아/어요** | Polite formal (everyday adult) | **해요** |
| **~습니다/세요** | Polite imperative | **하세요** |
| **~아/어** | Casual (close friends) | **해** |
| **~어/아 (반말)** | Plain | **해** |

Always use **~요** ending with strangers, older people, and in business. Use plain form only with close friends and children.

## 6. Titles & Honorifics

| Korean | Used for |
|:---|:---|
| **~님** | Very formal (고객님, 선생님) |
| **~씨** | Casual formal (김씨, Mark 씨) |
| **~이/가** | Subject marker (formal) |
| **형** | Older brother (male speaker) |
| **누나** | Older sister (male speaker) |
| **오빠** | Older brother (female speaker) |
| **언니** | Older sister (female speaker) |

> ⚠️ English speakers often miss honorifics. Always use **~요** endings with strangers — dropping them sounds rude.

## 7. Useful Phrases

| Korean | Pronunciation | English |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **감사합니다** | gamsahamnida | Thank you (formal) |
| **고마워요** | gomawoyo | Thanks (polite) |
| **고마워** | gomawa | Thanks (casual) |
| **죄송합니다** | joesonghamnida | I'm sorry (formal) |
| **미안해요** | mianheyo | Sorry (polite) |
| **네** | ne | Yes |
| **아니요** | anio | No |
| **괜찮아요** | gwaenchayo | It's okay / You're welcome |

## 8. Practical Tips

- ☑️ **Always greet first** — skipping greetings in Korea feels abrupt
- ☑️ **Bow slightly** when meeting — depth depends on formality
- ☑️ **Use two hands for business cards** (명함)
- ☑️ **Wait to be addressed first** by senior people in group settings
- ☑️ **Practice ~요 endings** — they're your safest default
