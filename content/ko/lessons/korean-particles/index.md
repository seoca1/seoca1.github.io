> 🇰🇷 **운영자용 한국어 요약**
> 1. 은/는 = topic marker (Topic, not new info): "As for X..."
> 2. 이/가 = subject marker (new info, identifier): "It is X that..."
> 3. 을/를 = object marker (direct object of verb): "I verb X"
> 4. ~에 = at/to, ~에서 = at/in/from, ~으로 = by/with/using
> 5. 의 = 's / of, ~(이)랑 = with/and, ~(이)나 = or

---

# Korean Particles (조사)

Korean particles are **small suffixes** attached to nouns to indicate their grammatical role. They replace English word order as the main structural device.

## 1. Topic Marker: 은 / 는

**은** and **는** mark the **topic** — what the sentence is *about*. Unlike English, the topic is often *known* information, with new information coming after.

| Last consonant | Particle | Example |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| vowel or ㄹ | **는** | 나**는** (I, topic), 물**은** doesn't exist (물 ends in ㄹ, so 는) |
| other consonant | **은** | 책**은** (book, topic), 한국**은** (Korea, topic) |

### Usage

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **저는 학생이에요.** | As for me, (I'm) a student. |
| **이 책은 재미있어요.** | As for this book, it's interesting. |
| **한국은 겨울이 추워요.** | As for Korea, winter is cold. |

> 💡 Think of 은/는 as **"Speaking of X..."** — it shifts the listener's focus to X.

## 2. Subject Marker: 이 / 가

**이** and **가** mark the **subject** — who/what performs the action.

| Last consonant | Particle | Example |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| vowel or ㄹ | **가** | 나**가** (I, subject), 물**이** doesn't apply |
| other consonant | **이** | 책**이** (book, subject), 한국**이** (Korea, subject) |

### Usage

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **고양이가 있어요.** | There is a cat. (cat = subject) |
| **누가 왔어요?** | Who came? (who = subject, new info) |
| **비가 와요.** | Rain is coming. (rain = subject) |

## 3. 은/는 vs 이/가 — When to Use Which

This is **the most confusing** part for English speakers:

| 은/는 (topic) | 이/가 (subject) |
|:---|:---|
| Topic = already known | Subject = new info, identifier |
| **책은** 읽었어요. (Topic: book — yes, the book, I've read it) | **책이** 좋아요. (Subject: book — books in general are good) |
| **저는** 학생이에요. (Topic: me — introducing myself) | **제가** 했어요. (Subject: I — I did it!) |

### Tricky examples
- **이 영화는** 재미있어요. = This movie (as topic) is fun.
- **이 영화가** 재미있어요. = This movie (as subject) is the fun one. (Comparing)
- **저는** 김치 먹어요. = I (topic) eat kimchi.
- **제가** 김치 먹었어요. = I (subject) ate the kimchi.

## 4. Object Marker: 을 / 를

**을** and **를** mark the **direct object** — what the verb acts upon.

| Last consonant | Particle | Example |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| vowel or ㄹ | **를** | 나**를** (me, object), 물**을** doesn't apply |
| other consonant | **을** | 책**을** (book, object), 한국**을** (Korea, object) |

### Examples

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **책을** 읽어요. | I read a book. |
| **커피를** 마셔요. | I drink coffee. |
| **한국어를** 공부해요. | I study Korean. |
| **김밥을** 먹었어요. | I ate kimbap. |

> In casual speech, 을/를 is often **dropped** — but in writing, always include it.

## 5. Time/Place Particles

### ~에 (at, to, on)
| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **학교에** 가요. | I go to school. |
| **3시에** 만나요. | Let's meet at 3. |
| **집에** 있어요. | I'm at home. |

### ~에서 (at, from, in)
| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **도서관에서** 공부해요. | I study at the library. |
| **한국에서** 왔어요. | I came from Korea. |

### ~에서 ~까지 (from ~ to ~)
- **9시에서 5시까지** 일해요. = I work from 9 to 5.

## 6. Instrument/Means: ~으로 / ~로

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **연필로** 쓰세요. | Write with a pencil. |
| **버스로** 가요. | I go by bus. |
| **한국어로** 말해요. | I speak in Korean. |

> After ㄹ: use **로** instead of 으로: 설탕**으로** → 설탕**로** (설탕 ends in ㅇ, so 으로, but 버스 ends in other consonants).

## 7. Other Useful Particles

| Particle | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **의** | 's / of | **한국의** 수도 (Korea's capital) |
| **도** | also / too | **저도** 가요. (I also go) |
| **만** | only | **커피만** 마셔요. (I drink only coffee) |
| **랑/이랑** | with / and | **친구랑** 같이 (with a friend) |
| **이나/나** | or | **커피나** 차 (coffee or tea) |

## 8. Word Order with Particles

Korean follows **SOV** (Subject-Object-Verb). Particles make this flexible:

```
저는  (Topic: I)
한국 음식을  (Object: Korean food, marked by 를)
먹어요.  (Verb: eat)
```

Without particles, this would be ambiguous:
- "저 한국 음식 먹어요" = ambiguous
- "**저는** 한국 **음식을** 먹어요" = clear

## 9. Practical Tips

- ☑️ **Master 은/는 vs 이/가 first** — it's the biggest hurdle
- ☑️ **을/를 can be skipped in speech** — 은/는 cannot
- ☑️ **Match the particle to the syllable's last sound** — vowel/ㄹ uses the first form, other consonants use the second
- ☑️ **Listen for particle sounds** — they signal who's doing what
- ☑️ **Practice with simple sentences** — Korean particles become intuitive with repetition
