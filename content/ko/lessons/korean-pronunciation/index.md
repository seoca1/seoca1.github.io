> 🇰🇷 **운영자용 한국어 요약**
> 1. 영어 화자가 어려워하는 발음: 경음화(된소리화), 연음, 비음화, 구개음화, ㅎ 탈락/添加
> 2. 경음화: 받침 ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ + 다음 음절 초성 ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅅ/ㅈ → 된소리 ㄲ/ㄸ/ㅃ/ㅆ/ㅉ
> 3. 연음: 받침 ㄱ/ㄴ/ㄹ/ㅁ/ㅇ + 모음 → 다음 음절 초성으로 이동 (e.g. 한국어 = 한구거)
> 4. 비음화: 받침 ㅂ/ㅁ/ㅍ + ㄴ/ㅁ → ㅁ/ㅂ + ㄴ → ㅇ (e.g. 입니다 → 임니다)
> 5. 구개음화: 받침 ㄷ/ㅌ + 이 → ㅈ/ㅊ (e.g. 같이 = 가치)

# Korean Pronunciation Rules

## 1. Sound System Overview

Korean has **19 consonant sounds** and **10 vowel sounds**, organized around three pillars:

| Feature | Korean | English Comparison |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Aspiration** (강한 기음) | ㅋ /ㅌ /ㅍ /ㅊ | "pin" vs "spin" — Korean marks this |
| **Tensification** (경음화) | ㄲ /ㄸ /ㅃ /ㅆ /ㅉ | Korean-only sound tightening |
| **Nasalization** (비음화) | ㅁ /ㄴ /ㅇ | Like English "m", "n", "ng" |

## 2. Five Major Pronunciation Rules

### Rule 1: 연음 (Yeon-eum / Liaison)

When a syllable ends in **ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅇ, ㅇ** and the next syllable starts with a vowel, the final consonant **moves to the next syllable**:

| Written | Pronounced | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 한국**어** | 한**구**거 | Korean (language) |
| 옷**이** | 오**시** | clothes (subject marker) |
| 맛**있**어요 | 마**시**써요 | It's delicious |

### Rule 2: 경음화 (Gyeong-eumhwa / Tensification)

When **ㄱ, ㄷ, ㅂ, ㅅ, ㅈ** appear as 받침 and the next syllable starts with ㄱ, ㄷ, ㅂ, ㅅ, ㅈ, they become **tense** (된소리):

| Written | Pronounced | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 국**보** | 국**뽀** | national treasure |
| 있**게** | 일**께** | so that (there is) |
| 낮**에** | 나**제** | in the afternoon |

> This is the hardest rule for English speakers — you must **tense your throat** for the second consonant.

### Rule 3: 비음화 (Bi-eumhwa / Nasalization)

When **ㅂ, ㅍ, ㄹ(ㄴ case)** meet **ㄴ** as the next syllable's onset, the **ㄴ becomes ㅇ**:

| Written | Pronounced | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 입니다 | 임**니**다 | is (formal) |
| 한글**날** | 한글**날** | Hangul Day (no change — special case) |
| 샴**푸** | 삼**푸** | shampoo |

### Rule 4: 구개음화 (Gu-gae-eumhwa / Palatalization)

When **ㄷ, ㅌ, ㅅ, ㅆ, ㅈ, ㅊ** as 받침 meet **이** as the next onset, they become **ㅈ, ㅊ, ㅊ, ㅊ, ㅉ, ㅊ**:

| Written | Pronounced | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 같**이** | 가**치** | together |
| 굳**이** | 구**지** | deliberately |
| 낟**이** | 나**지** | grain (archaic) |

### Rule 5: ㅎ Special Cases

The letter **ㅎ** behaves uniquely:

| Pattern | Rule | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| ㅎ + ㄴ/ㄹ/ㅁ → ㅎ drops | 좋아요 + 네 → 조아요네 → **조아네** |
| ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅅ/ㅈ + ㅎ → fortition | 많**하** → 마**파** |
| ㅎ + vowel → silent or aspirated | 좋**아**요 = 조아요 (no aspiration here) |

## 3. Common Trouble Spots for English Speakers

| English speakers say | Korean says | Why |
|:---|:---|:---|
| "seoul" /soʊl/ | 서울 /sʌ.ul/ | ㅓ is between "uh" and "uh" |
| "kimchi" /kɪmtʃi/ | 김치 /kim.tɕʰi/ | ㄱ=soft g, ㅊ=aspirated ch |
| "bap" /bæp/ | 밥 /pap/ (or /bap/) | ㅂ can be unvoiced between vowels |
| "annyeong" /æn.jʌŋ/ | 안녕 /an.njʌŋ/ | ㅇ = silent at start, "ng" at end |

> 💡 Most Korean speakers will understand your English-style pronunciation — these rules just help you sound more natural.

## 4. Syllable Stress

Korean is **syllable-timed**, not stress-timed like English:

- English: every word has one stressed syllable (e.g., "im-PORT-ant")
- Korean: each syllable gets roughly equal weight

This is why Korean can sound "machine-gun" like to English speakers — and why English sounds musical to Korean speakers.

## 5. Intonation

Korean uses **pitch** (not stress) to convey questions:

- Statement: 안**녕**하세요 (annyeong-haseyo) — pitch goes down
- Question: 안**녕**하세요? — pitch goes up at the end

Otherwise, Korean intonation is fairly flat compared to English.

## 6. Practical Tips

- ☑️ **Listen to K-dramas / K-pop** for natural rhythm
- ☑️ **Practice 받침 rules out loud** — they're the most counterintuitive
- ☑️ **Record yourself** and compare with native audio
- ☑️ **Don't worry about being perfect** — even imperfect Korean is appreciated
- ☑️ **Watch your mouth shape** — Korean has more rounded lips than English
