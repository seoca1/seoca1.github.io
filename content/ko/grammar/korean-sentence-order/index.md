# Korean Sentence Order

## 1. SOV — The Basic Structure

Korean is **Subject-Object-Verb**. English is **Subject-Verb-Object**. This shift changes how you think about sentences.

| English (SVO) | Korean (SOV) |
|:---|:---|
| **I** (S) eat (V) **kimchi** (O) | **저는** 김치**를** 먹**어요** |
| **She** (S) reads (V) **books** (O) | **그녀는** 책**을** 읽**어요** |
| **They** (S) drink (V) **coffee** (O) | **그들은** 커피**를** 마셔**요** |

Notice: the **verb always comes last**. Once you know the verb, the sentence is "over" in Korean.

## 2. Time — Place — Manner

When adding adverbs (time, place, manner), Korean follows a strict order:

```
Time → Place → Manner → Verb
```

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **어제** (Time) **학교에서** (Place) **친구와** (Manner) 만났어요. | I met my friend at school yesterday. |
| **내일** (Time) **서울에서** (Place) **비행기로** (Manner) 가요. | I'll go to Seoul tomorrow by plane. |
| **매일** (Time) **집에서** (Place) **한국어로** (Manner) 공부해요. | I study Korean at home every day. |

This order is **strict** — moving time and place around sounds unnatural.

## 3. Modifiers Come BEFORE the Noun

Korean adjectives and relative clauses **precede** the noun they modify:

| English (modifier after) | Korean (modifier before) |
|:---|:---|
| a **big** house | **큰** 집 |
| a **red** apple | **빨간** 사과 |
| the **book that I read** | **제가 읽은** 책 |
| the **person who came** | **온** 사람 |

### Adjective + noun pattern
- **예쁜** 꽃 = pretty flower
- **맛있는** 음식 = delicious food
- **비싼** 차 = expensive car

### Relative clause (관형절)
To make a relative clause, conjugate the verb in **adnominal form** (관형형):

| Tense | Adnominal | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Present | ~는 | **먹는** 사람 = person who eats |
| Past | ~ㄴ/은 | **먹은** 음식 = food (that was) eaten |
| Future | ~ㄹ/을 | **먹을** 음식 = food (that will be) eaten |

### After vowels: ~ㄴ
- **예쁜** 꽃 = pretty flower (from 예쁘다, drop 다 → 예쁘 → add ㄴ)

### After consonants: ~은
- **작은** 집 = small house (from 작다, drop 다 → 작 → add 은)

## 4. Topic-Comment Structure

Korean often uses **은/는** to set a topic, then comment on it:

```
[Topic: 은/는] [Comment: rest of sentence]
```

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **저는** 학생이에요. | As for me, (I'm) a student. |
| **한국은** 겨울이 추워요. | As for Korea, winter is cold. |
| **이 책은** 재미있어요. | As for this book, it's interesting. |

This lets Korean **front** important information regardless of grammatical role.

## 5. Connectors and Conjunctions

| Word | Function | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **그리고** | and | 커피**그리고** 빵 (coffee and bread) |
| **~고** | and then | 먹**고** 마셔요 (eat and then drink) |
| **하지만** | but | 비싸**지만** 좋아요 (expensive but good) |
| **그래서** | so/therefore | 비가 와요. **그래서** 집에 있어요. (rain falls, so stay home) |
| **왜냐하면** | because | **왜냐하면** 비가 와서 (because it's raining) |
| **~때문에** | because of | 비 **때문에** (because of rain) |

## 6. Question Words

| Korean | Position | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **누구** (who) | anywhere except last | **누구**가 왔어요? |
| **무엇/뭐** (what) | same | **뭐** 먹어요? |
| **언제** (when) | often beginning | **언제** 가요? |
| **어디** (where) | often beginning | **어디** 살아요? |
| **왜** (why) | often beginning | **왜** 안 와요? |
| **어떻게** (how) | same | **어떻게** 가요? |

## 7. Common Word Order Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✓ Correct | Why |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 먹어요 김치를 | 김치를 먹어요 | Verb last |
| 빨간 (after noun) | 빨간 사과 | Modifier first |
| 학교 어제 가요 | 어제 학교에 가요 | Time first |

## 8. Practical Tips

- ☑️ **Always end with the verb** — it's your anchor
- ☑️ **Place time first** in longer sentences
- ☑️ **Pre-modify** with relative clauses (V + noun)
- ☑️ **Topic first** with 은/는 when comparing or contrasting
- ☑️ **Practice by translating** English SVO → Korean SOV daily
