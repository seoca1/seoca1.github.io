> 🇰🇷 **운영자용 한국어 요약**
> 1. 가족 호칭은 **화자의 시점** + **성별**에 따라 다름 (오빠/형, 누나/언니)
> 2. 친/외 = 친정(아버지 쪽) / 외가(어머니 쪽): 친할머니 vs 외할머니
> 3. 시/처 = 남편 쪽 / 아내 쪽: 시어머니 vs 장모
> 4. 큰/작은 = 연장/연소: 큰아버지 vs 작은아버지
> 5. 손위/손아래 = 연장/연소 (친척 전반): 손위 = older cousin, 손아래 = younger

# Korean Family Vocabulary

Korean family terms are **highly specific** — they change based on **the speaker's gender, age, and side of the family**.

## 1. Immediate Family (Immediate = 본인/배우자/부모/자녀)

### Parents

| Korean | English | Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **아버지** | father (formal) | speaking TO or ABOUT (formal) |
| **아빠** | dad (casual) | close family only |
| **어머니** | mother (formal) | same as above |
| **엄마** | mom (casual) | |
| **부모님** | parents (honorific) | when addressing |

### Spouse

| Korean | English | Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **남편** | husband (your own) | female speaker |
| **아내** | wife (your own) | male speaker |
| **남자친구** | boyfriend | |
| **여자친구** | girlfriend | |
| **배우자** | spouse (formal) | |

### Children

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **아들** | son |
| **딸** | daughter |
| **자녀** | children (formal) |
| **아이 / 어린이** | child |

## 2. Siblings — Speaker-Dependent

This is where it gets interesting. **형/오빠/누나/언니** depend on who is speaking:

### If you are a **male speaker**:

| Relation | Term |
|:---|:---|
| Older brother | **형** (hyeong) |
| Older sister | **누나** (nuna) |
| Younger brother | **남동생** (namdongsaeng) |
| Younger sister | **여동생** (yeodongsaeng) |

### If you are a **female speaker**:

| Relation | Term |
|:---|:---|
| Older brother | **오빠** (oppa) |
| Older sister | **언니** (eonni) |
| Younger brother | **남동생** (namdongsaeng) |
| Younger sister | **여동생** (yeodongsaeng) |

> 💡 Note: **오빠/언니** are sometimes used romantically (girlfriend to boyfriend) — context matters!

## 3. Grandparents

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **할아버지** | grandfather |
| **할머니** | grandmother |
| **조부모님** | grandparents (honorific) |

> **외 (oe)** = maternal side, **친 (chin)** = paternal side (mostly for aunts/uncles/grandparents):
> - **외할아버지** = maternal grandfather
> - **친할아버지** = paternal grandfather

## 4. Uncles and Aunts

| Korean | English | Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **삼촌** | uncle (father's side) | also 친삼촌 |
| **외삼촌** | uncle (mother's side) | |
| **이모** | aunt (mother's side) | mother's sister |
| **고모** | aunt (father's side) | father's sister |

> Note: 큰/작은 = older/younger: **큰아버지** (older uncle of father), **작은아버지** (younger uncle of father).

## 5. In-Laws (시/처)

| Korean | English | Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **시어머니** | mother-in-law (husband's side) | husband's mother |
| **시아버지** | father-in-law (husband's side) | husband's father |
| **장모** | mother-in-law (wife's side) | wife's mother |
| **장인** | father-in-law (wife's side) | wife's father |
| **며느리** | daughter-in-law | |
| **사위** | son-in-law | |

> ⚠️ 시 vs 처: **시 = husband's side** (시어머니), **처 = wife's side** (처갓집).

## 6. Cousins

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **사촌** | cousin (general) |
| **사촌 형/오빠/누나/언니** | older cousin (uses speaker-dependent terms!) |
| **사촌 남동생/여동생** | younger cousin |

| Side | Term |
|:---|:---|
| Father's brother's child | **친사촌** (cousin) |
| Mother's side cousins | **외사촌** |

## 7. Speaking vs. Referring

Korean has **two versions** of most family terms:

| Speaking to/about someone your age (or below) | Speaking about someone older / to someone else |
|:---|:---|
| **여동생** (to my sister) | **누이** (literary) |
| **오빠** (to my brother) | **형** (3rd person) |

> When talking **about** your family to others, you can use the same casual forms, OR use **제 (my, formal)** to show respect: **제 오빠** = my (older) brother.

## 8. Possessive — 내 vs 제

| Form | Use | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **내** | informal, close family | **내** 동생 |
| **제** | formal | **제** 가족 |

## 9. Practical Examples

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **제 가족이에요.** | This is my family. |
| **오빠가 두 명 있어요.** | I have two older brothers. |
| **외할머니가 서울에 사세요.** | My maternal grandmother lives in Seoul. |
| **시어머니랑 같이 살아요.** | I live with my mother-in-law. |
| **형은 회사원이에요.** | My (older) brother is an office worker. |
| **누나는 의사예요.** | My (older) sister is a doctor. |

## 10. Practical Tips

- ☑️ **Learn speaker-dependent terms together** — 형/오빠, 누나/언니
- ☑️ **Distinguish 시 (husband's side) vs 처 (wife's side)** — easy to confuse
- ☑️ **Use 외/친 for grandparent/aunt side** — often skipped in casual
- ☑️ **큰/작은 for older/younger uncles** — common in traditional families
- ☑️ **Practice: 가족 소개 (family introductions)** — common conversation topic
