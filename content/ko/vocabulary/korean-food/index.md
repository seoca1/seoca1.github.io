## 3. Ordering Vocabulary

### Counting (인분 = servings)

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|

## 4. The Six Korean Tastes

Korean cuisine recognizes **6 tastes** (오미 + 맛), unlike English's basic 4:

| Korean | English | Symbol |
|:---|:---|:---:|
| **맵다** (maepda) | spicy | 🌶️ |
| **달다** (dalda) | sweet | 🍬 |
| **쓰다** (sseuda) | bitter | ☕ |
| **시다** (sida) | sour | 🍋 |
| **짭짤하다** (japjalhada) | salty | 🧂 |
| **고소하다** (gosohada) | nutty / savory | 🥜 |

### Taste Phrases

| Korean | English |
|:---|:---|
| **매워요** | It's spicy |
| **너무 매워요!** | Too spicy! |
| **달아요** | It's sweet |
| **너무 짜요** | Too salty |
| **맛있어요** | It's delicious |
| **맛없어요** | It's not good |
| **고소해요** | It's nutty/savory |

### Korean Table Manners

1. **Wait for elders to start eating** before you begin
2. **Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice** (resembles funeral incense)
3. **Use both hands** when pouring drinks for elders
4. **Turn cups around** if someone senior is pouring for you
5. **Say "잘 먹겠습니다"** before eating (formal: "I will eat well")
6. **Say "잘 먹었습니다"** after eating (formal: "I ate well")
7. **Don't pass food chopstick-to-chopstick** (funeral ritual)

## 8. Practical Tips

- ☑️ **반찬은 무료 리필** — don't be shy
- ☑️ **Tipping is not customary** in Korea
- ☑️ **Say 잘 먹겠습니다 before eating** — politeness matters
- ☑️ **Try different kimchi** types — there are 200+ varieties
- ☑️ **Ask "추천 메뉴 뭐예요?"** — recommendations are usually the chef's specialty
- ☑️ **Restaurants close early** — most stop serving after 9 PM
