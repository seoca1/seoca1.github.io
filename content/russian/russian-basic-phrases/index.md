## What Is Basic Vocabulary?

Russian basic vocabulary follows a **different pattern from Korean/Japanese/Chinese**:
- Numbers: must memorize 1-20 (Latin-origin root words)
- Days/Months: some words resemble Korean phonetically
- Adjectives: change by gender (masculine / feminine / neuter)

## Top 5 Mistakes by Korean Speakers

1. **Adjective gender agreement** — Korean adjectives don't inflect, but Russian adjectives change for masc/fem/neut. Red = Красный/Красная/Красное.
2. **Number + hour noun agreement** — час/часа/часов change by number. Must be memorized.
3. **40 (сорок), 90 (девяносто)** — irregular patterns. Must be memorized.
4. **Case declension** — adjectives/verbs inflect by noun gender. Separate study required.
5. **Р pronunciation** — tongue trill. Hard to pronounce every word accurately.
