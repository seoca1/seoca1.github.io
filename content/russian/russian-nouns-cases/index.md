# Russian Noun Case Declension

> 🇰🇷 **한국어 요약 (Korean Summary)**
> - **핵심:** Russian has **6 noun cases** — like Korean particles, nouns change form. The hardest part of Russian.
> - **Korean difference:** Korean particles (을/를/이/가) attach to the unchanged noun. Russian: the noun itself changes (case endings).
> - **6 cases:** Nominative / Genitive / Dative / Accusative / Instrumental / Prepositional
> - **Gender:** masculine / feminine / neuter — case patterns differ by gender.
> - **Next steps:** adjective declension + verb conjugation

---

## What Is Case Declension?

In Russian, nouns change form based on their role in a sentence, similar to Korean particles. But while Korean keeps the noun intact and attaches a particle (e.g., "책을"), Russian **changes the noun itself** (e.g., книга → книги).

**Korean vs Russian comparison:**

| Korean | Russian |
|:---|:---|
| 책을 봤다 | Я вижу книг**у** |
| 책이 있다 | У меня есть книг**а** |
| 책에 썼다 | Я пишу книг**ой** |
| 책에서 왔다 | Я пришёл из книг**и** |

> **Key point:** Korean = unchanged noun + particle. Russian = the noun itself changes.

## The 6 Cases (Шесть падежей)

| # | Case (Падеж) | Korean Equivalent | Main Function |
|:--:|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | **Именительный** (Nominative) | 주격 (~이/가) | subject, dictionary form |
| 2 | **Родительный** (Genitive) | 속격 (~의) | possession, absence |
| 3 | **Дательный** (Dative) | 여격 (~에게/에) | indirect object |
| 4 | **Винительный** (Accusative) | 대격 (~을/를) | direct object |
| 5 | **Творительный** (Instrumental) | 조격 (~으로/와) | instrument, accompaniment |
| 6 | **Предложный** (Prepositional) | 전치격 (~에서/에) | location, topic |

> **Korean learner comparison:** Maps closely to the 6 Korean particle categories (이/가, 을/를, 에/에게, 의, 로/와, 에서/에).

## Noun Genders

Case patterns differ by **noun gender**. Identify gender by the noun's ending:

| Gender | Ending | Example | Korean |
|:---:|:---:|:---|:---|
| Masculine (Мужской род) | consonant or -ь | стол (table), дом (house) | 남자 |
| Feminine (Женский род) | -а, -я | книга (book), земля (land) | 여자 |
| Neuter (Средний род) | -о, -е | окно (window), море (sea) | 중성 |

> **Korean learner note:** Korean nouns have no gender. Russian nouns are masc/fem/neuter. You must memorize gender first.

## Masculine Noun Pattern (consonant ending)

Example: стол (table, masculine)

| Case | Ending | Form | Example Sentence | Korean |
|:--:|:---:|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 (Nom) | — | стол | Это стол. | 이건 테이블이야 |
| 2 (Gen) | -а | стола | У меня нет стола. | 테이블이 없어 |
| 3 (Dat) | -у | столу | Я иду к столу. | 테이블에 가요 |
| 4 (Acc) | — (no change if consonant) | стол | Я вижу стол. | 테이블 봐 |
| 5 (Ins) | -ом | столом | Я работаю со столом. | 테이블로 일해요 |
| 6 (Prep) | -е | столе | Я думаю о столе. | 테이블에 대해 생각해요 |

> **Rule:** Masculine nouns (consonant ending) have Accusative = Nominative (no change).

### Masculine Animate (people/animals)

For human/animal masculine nouns, Accusative = Genitive:

| Case | Ending | Form | Example |
|:--:|:---:|:---|:---|
| 1 | — | студент (student) | Это студент. |
| 2 | -а | студента | У меня нет студента. |
| 3 | -у | студенту | Я иду к студенту. |
| 4 (= 2) | -а | студента | Я вижу студента. |
| 5 | -ом | студентом | Я работаю со студентом. |
| 6 | -е | студенте | Я думаю о студенте. |

## Feminine Noun Pattern (-а/-я)

Example: книга (book, feminine)

| Case | Ending | Form | Example Sentence | Korean |
|:--:|:---:|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | -а | книга | Это книга. | 이건 책이야 |
| 2 | -ы/-и | книги | У меня нет книги. | 책이 없어 |
| 3 | -е | книге | Я иду к книге. | 책에 가요 |
| 4 | -у/-ю | книгу | Я вижу книгу. | 책 봐 |
| 5 | -ой/-ей | книгой | Я работаю с книгой. | 책으로 일해요 |
| 6 | -е | книге | Я думаю о книге. | 책에 대해 생각해요 |

> **Rule:** Feminine Genitive = -ы (after consonant) / -и (after г/к/х/ж/ш/щ/ч).

### Feminine Variant — земля (land)

| Case | Ending | Form |
|:--:|:---:|:---|
| 1 | -я | земля |
| 2 | -и | земли |
| 3 | -е | земле |
| 4 | -ю | землю |
| 5 | -ёй/-ей | землёй |
| 6 | -е | земле |

## Neuter Noun Pattern (-о/-е)

Example: окно (window, neuter)

| Case | Ending | Form | Example Sentence | Korean |
|:--:|:---:|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | -о | окно | Это окно. | 이건 창문이야 |
| 2 | -а | окна | У меня нет окна. | 창문이 없어 |
| 3 | -у | окну | Я иду к окну. | 창문에 가요 |
| 4 (= 1) | -о | окно | Я вижу окно. | 창문 봐 |
| 5 | -ом | окном | Я работаю с окном. | 창문으로 일해요 |
| 6 | -е | окне | Я думаю о окне. | 창문에 대해 생각해요 |

> **Rule:** Neuter Accusative = Nominative. Same as Korean "~을/를".

## Korean vs Russian Case Mapping

| Korean Particle | Russian Case | Example (Korean) | Example (Russian) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| ~이/가 (subject) | Nom | 책이 | книга (1st case) |
| ~의 (possessive) | Gen | 책의 | книги (2nd case) |
| ~에게/에 (indirect) | Dat | 책에게/책에 | книге (3rd case) |
| ~을/를 (direct) | Acc | 책을 | книгу (4th case) |
| ~으로/와 (instrument/companion) | Ins | 책으로/책과 | книгой (5th case) |
| ~에서/에 (location) | Prep | 책에서/책에 | книге (6th case) |

## Practical Sentences by Case

### Nominative — Subject

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Книга интересная. | Kniga interesnaya. | 책이 흥미롭다 |
| Студент умный. | Student umnyy. | 학생이 똑똑하다 |

### Genitive — Possession / Absence

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean |
|:---|:---|:---|
| У меня нет книги. | U menya net knigi. | 내 책이 없다 |
| Это книга студента. | Eto kniga studenta. | 이건 학생의 책이다 |

> **Structure:** У + possessor + есть + noun (possession)
> **Absence:** У + possessor + нет + noun in Genitive

### Dative — Indirect Object

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Я даю книгу студенту. | Ya dayu knigu studentu. | 학생에게 책을 준다 |
| Я пишу письмо другу. | Ya pishu pis'mo drugu. | 친구에게 편지를 쓴다 |

### Accusative — Direct Object

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Я вижу книгу. | Ya vizhu knigu. | 책을 본다 |
| Я читаю книгу. | Ya chitayu knigu. | 책을 읽는다 |

### Instrumental — Instrument / Accompaniment

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Я пишу ручкой. | Ya pishu ruchkoy. | 펜으로 쓴다 |
| Я иду с другом. | Ya idu s drugom. | 친구와 간다 |

> **Accompaniment:** с + Instrumental. Same as Korean "~와/과".

### Prepositional — Location / Topic

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Я живу в Москве. | Ya zhivu v Moskve. | 모스크바에 살아 |
| Я думаю о книге. | Ya dumayu o knige. | 책에 대해 생각해 |

> **Location:** в + Prepositional. Same as Korean "~에".
> **Topic:** о + Prepositional. Same as Korean "~에 대해/관하여".

## Top 5 Mistakes by Korean Speakers

1. **Ignoring declension** — noun itself changes. You must memorize case endings.
2. **Confusing gender** — nouns have masc/fem/neuter. Book = книга (feminine), table = стол (masculine).
3. **Acc = Nom vs Acc = Gen confusion** — masculine inanimate (consonant ending) Acc = Nom (no change); masculine animate Acc = Gen (-а).
4. **Gen vs Prep ending identical (-е)** — Dat/Prep both use -е. Disambiguate by context/preposition.
5. **У меня есть (possession)** — word order differs from Korean "내가 ~가 있다". Structure: У + person + есть + noun.

## 5-Step Learning Path

### Level 1 — Nominative (Case 1)
Noun dictionary form + subject sentences.

### Level 2 — Genitive (Case 2)
Possession expressions (У меня есть/нет).

### Level 3 — Accusative (Case 4)
Direct object (~을/를).

### Level 4 — Instrumental (Case 5)
Instrument/accompaniment expressions (с + Ins).

### Level 5 — Dative (Case 3) + Prepositional (Case 6)
Indirect object / location / topic.
