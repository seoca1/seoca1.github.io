# Russian Pronunciation

> 🇰🇷 **한국어 요약 (Korean Summary)**
> - **핵심:** Russian pronunciation revolves around **stress (accent)** + **assimilation** + **softening (palatalization)**.
> - **Korean comparison:** Korean pronunciation is comparatively uniform, while Russian has vowel reduction/dropping depending on stress position.
> - **Korean learner notes:** Р (tongue trill), Ж/Ш (rounded lips), Х (velar fricative), В (labiodental fricative) — sounds absent in Korean.
> - **Softening rule:** consonant + Ь = soft articulation (different from Korean liaison rules).

## What Are Pronunciation Rules?

Russian follows a **phonemic orthography** — what you write is largely what you pronounce. However, three core rules govern accurate pronunciation:

1. **Stress (ударение, accent)** — placement of the emphasized syllable
2. **Assimilation (ассимиляция)** — consonant voicing/devoicing across word boundaries
3. **Softening (смягчение)** — palatalization triggered by ь

## 1. Stress (ударение)

Russian **stress position** is critical. Stress placement causes:
- Vowel quality to change (stressed vowels are clear, unstressed vowels are reduced)
- Word meaning to change (minimal pairs differ only in stress)

### Stress Marking

Russian dictionaries and textbooks mark stress with an **´ (acute accent)** symbol:

| Spelling | Pronunciation | Stress Position |
|:---|:---|:---:|
| мама́ | ma-MA | 2nd syllable |
| до́ма | DO-ma | 1st syllable |
| молоко́ | ma-la-KO | 3rd syllable |

> **Rule:** The stressed vowel is long and clear; all other vowels are short and reduced.

### Korean Learner Notes — Stress Acquisition

| Korean Learner Challenge | Reason | Solution |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Don't know stress position | Korean has no lexical stress concept | Memorize stress per word |
| Vowel reduction | Korean vowels are uniform | Treat unstressed vowels as a schwa-like "uh/ih" |
| Stress shift in inflected forms | Declensions can move stress | Memorize stress per grammatical form |

## 2. Assimilation — Consonant Mutation

Like Korean, Russian has **liaison/assimilation** rules. Consonants change across word boundaries.

### Devoicing (оглушение)

**Korean rule:** Word-final voiced consonant → voiceless before next word's initial vowel.
**Russian rule:** Same! Word-final voiced consonant → voiceless before next word's initial voiceless consonant.

| Original | Pronunciation Change | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| дуб (oak) | дуб + берёза → дуп-берёза | "b" before "b" → devoiced |
| мороз (frost) | мороз + снег → мороск + снег | "з" before "с" → devoiced |

> **Korean learner comparison:** Like Korean "밥을" → "바블" (liaison), Russian pronunciation shifts naturally at word boundaries.

### Voicing (озвончение)

In Russian, **voiceless + voiced** = voicing (reverse direction):

| Original | Pronunciation Change | Explanation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| просьба | про́з-ба | с + ь → з + б (softening + voicing) |
| футбол | фуд-бол | т + б → д + б |

## 3. Softening (смягчение) — Consonant + ь

The Russian ь (soft sign) **is not pronounced itself** — instead, it makes the preceding consonant **soft (palatalized)**.

| Spelling | Pronunciation | Comparison |
|:---|:---|:---|
| мат (rug) | [mɑt] | hard т |
| мать (mother) | [mɑtʲ] | soft т (т + ь) |
| брат (brother) | [brɑt] | hard т |
| брать (to take) | [brɑtʲ] | soft т (т + ь) |

> **Korean learner comparison:** Korean "같이" = 가 + 치 (palatalization triggered naturally). Similarly т + ь = natural palatalization.

### Softening by Vowel Context

| Vowel | Hard Form | Soft Form |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| а | мат /mɑt/ | мать /mɑtʲ/ |
| е | нет /nʲet/ | неть /nʲetʲ/ |
| и | нос /nos/ | носить /nasʲitʲ/ |
| о | дом /dom/ | — (auto-soft after vowel) |
| у | тук /tuk/ | — |

> **Rule:** Consonants are automatically palatalized before е, и, ю, я, ё. The ь marker is unnecessary in those cases.

## 4. Voiced / Voiceless Pairs

Russian distinguishes voiced and voiceless consonants, like Korean (e.g., Korean "밥" vs "팝"):

| Voiced | Voiceless | Korean Comparison |
|:---:|:---:|:---|
| Б б | П п | ㅂ vs ㅍ |
| В в | Ф ф | (no Korean equivalent) |
| Г г | К к | ㄱ vs ㅋ |
| Д д | Т т | ㄷ vs ㅌ |
| Ж ж | Ш ш | (no Korean equivalent) |
| З з | С с | (no Korean equivalent) |

## 5. Sounds Korean Learners Struggle With

### Р (Russian "R") — Tongue Trill

Different from Korean ㄹ:
- Korean ㄹ: tongue tip lightly touches alveolar ridge
- Russian Р: tongue tip vibrates against alveolar ridge (trilled)

| Word | Transliteration | Korean Learner Attempt |
|:---|:---|:---|
| рука | ruka | tongue-tip trill → "ru-ka" |
| русский | russkiy | tongue-tip trill → "rus-skiy" |

### Ж / Ш (Postalveolar Fricatives)

Different from Korean ㅈ / ㅅ:
- Round your lips (rounded)
- Tongue tip approaches the postalveolar region (front of alveolar ridge)

| Word | Transliteration | Korean Learner Attempt |
|:---|:---|:---|
| жизнь | zhizn' | rounded lips → "zhiz-n'" |
| школа | shkola | rounded lips → "shko-la" |

### Х (Velar Fricative)

Different from Korean ㅎ:
- Tongue back + velar fricative
- Korean ㅎ: glottal fricative (loose friction)
- Russian Х: velar fricative (strong friction, deeper)

| Word | Transliteration | Korean Learner Attempt |
|:---|:---|:---|
| хлеб | khleb | back-tongue friction → "khleb" |
| хорошо | khorosho | "kho-ro-sho" |

### В (Labiodental Fricative)

Different from Korean ㅂ:
- Upper teeth touch lower lip lightly + fricative

| Word | Transliteration | Korean Learner Attempt |
|:---|:---|:---|
| вода | voda | upper teeth + lower lip → "vo-da" |
| ваш | vash | "vash" |

## Top 5 Mistakes by Korean Speakers

1. **Ignoring stress** — pronouncing every syllable equally. Memorize stress positions and unstressed vowel reduction.
2. **Pronouncing Р like Korean ㄹ** — tongue trill required. Korean ㄹ-style soft touch will sound wrong.
3. **Pronouncing Ж/Ш like Korean ㅈ/ㅅ** — round your lips. Flat Korean-style pronunciation sounds unnatural.
4. **Pronouncing Х like Korean ㅎ** — velar fricative. Loose Korean ㅎ-style pronunciation is incorrect.
5. **Ignoring Ь** — ь marks palatalization. When pronouncing "мать", the "т" must be palatalized.
