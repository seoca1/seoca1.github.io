# Russian Greetings + Introductions

> 🇰🇷 **한국어 요약 (Korean Summary)**
> - **핵심:** 20 core Russian greetings + self-introduction patterns. Direct counterparts to Korean "안녕하세요/감사합니다".
> - **Structure:** No time-of-day greetings (Russian), but formal/informal distinction by situation.
> - **Korean learner comparison:** Привет = Korean "안녕" (casual), Здравствуйте = "안녕하세요" (formal). One word for any time of day.
> - **Self-introduction:** Меня зовут... (My name is...) + Я из... (I come from...).
> - **Next steps:** Russian_Basic_Phrases + advanced vocabulary

## 20 Basic Greetings

Unlike Korean/Japanese, Russian **does not distinguish greetings by time of day**. "Здравствуйте" works for both morning and evening.

### Most Common Greetings

| Russian | Pronunciation | Korean | Formality |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| Привет | Privet | 안녕 | Casual |
| Здравствуйте | Zdrastvuyte | 안녕하세요 | Formal |
| Здравствуй | Zdrastvuy | 안녕 (semi-formal) | Formal |
| Добрый день | Dobryy den' | 좋은 하루 | Formal |

> **Key:** Привет (friend) vs Здравствуйте (formal). Similar to Korean 너/당신 distinction.

## Formal vs Casual (Ты / Вы)

Russian has a **2-tier formality system** similar to Korean:

| Form | Used For | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Ты (ty) | Friends, family, close people | Ты как? (How are you?) |
| Вы (vy) | Elders, superiors, strangers | Вы как? (How are you, formal?) |

> **Korean learner tip:** Same as Korean 너/당신 distinction. For a stranger elder, use Вы.

## Self-Introduction Pattern

### Basic Structure

```
Меня зовут + [name]
Я из + [country/city]
Я + [occupation/title]
```

### Example

> **Меня зовут Иван. Я из России. Я студент.**
> (Menya zovut Ivan. Ya iz Rossii. Ya student.)
> (My name is Ivan. I'm from Russia. I'm a student.)

## Conversation Scenario: First Meeting

> **A:** Здравствуйте! Меня зовут Иван.
> (Zdrastvuyte! Menya zovut Ivan.)
> (Hello! My name is Ivan.)
>
> **B:** Здравствуйте! Я Сергей. Очень приятно!
> (Zdrastvuyte! Ya Sergey. Ochen' priyatno!)
> (Hello! I'm Sergey. Nice to meet you!)
>
> **A:** Вы из России?
> (Vy iz Rossii?)
> (Are you from Russia?)
>
> **B:** Да, я из Москвы. А вы?
> (Da, ya iz Moskvy. A vy?)
> (Yes, I'm from Moscow. And you?)
>
> **A:** Я из Кореи. Я студент.
> (Ya iz Korei. Ya student.)
> (I'm from Korea. I'm a student.)

## Top 5 Mistakes by Korean Speakers

1. **Time-of-day greetings** — "Good morning" Доброе утро is only for morning. Using it in the afternoon sounds odd.
2. **Confusing Ты/Вы** — Using Ты with a newly-met elder is rude. Default to Вы.
3. **Р pronunciation** — tongue trill required. Korean ㄹ-style pronunciation sounds unnatural.
4. **Спасибо formality** — Friends use Спасибо. In formal situations, prefer Большое спасибо.
5. **Привет ≠ "안녕하세요"** — Привет is casual. Formal situations require Здравствуйте.

Меня зовут + name / Я из + country / Я + occupation

Practice first-meeting scenarios

## Role Play: First Meeting (10 minutes)

### Scenario A — Self-Introduction
- **My role:** Speaker A
- **Partner role:** Speaker B
- **Situation:** First meeting. Ask/answer name, nationality, occupation, age.

### Scenario B — Formal Meeting
- **My role:** Formal partner (using Вы)
- **Partner role:** Formal host (using Вы)
- **Situation:** Business first meeting.
