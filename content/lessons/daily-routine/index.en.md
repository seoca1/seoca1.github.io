# Daily Routine (Rutina Diaria)



## Lesson Overview

This lesson teaches how to describe your daily routine in Spanish. It focuses on **reflexive verbs** — the grammatical backbone of everyday self-action descriptions — and the **present tense** conjugation patterns that apply across all Spanish verbs.

**Goal:** By the end of this lesson, you can describe your morning, daytime, and evening routines in 5–10 coherent Spanish sentences.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

1. Conjugate reflexive verbs in the present tense across all 6 persons
2. Use 9 core daily-activity verbs in context
3. Tell time in Spanish
4. Use time-of-day adverbs (morning, afternoon, evening)
5. Describe a complete daily routine in 5–10 sentences

---

## Core Grammar: Reflexive Verbs

**Reflexive verbs** describe actions a subject performs **on themselves**. They are essential for daily routines because most routine actions (waking up, showering, dressing) are self-directed.

For the full grammatical breakdown, see [[Reflexive_Verbs]]. Quick recap:

```
reflexive pronoun + conjugated verb (-se)
```

### The 6 reflexive pronouns

| Pronoun | Person | Meaning |
|:---:|:---:|:---|
| me | 1st sg. | myself |
| te | 2nd sg. | yourself (tú) |
| se | 3rd sg. | him/her/yourself (Ud.) |
| nos | 1st pl. | ourselves |
| os | 2nd pl. | yourselves (vosotros) |
| se | 3rd pl. | themselves/you all (Uds.) |

## Essential Daily Verbs

| Spanish | 1st person | English | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **levantarse** | me levanto | to get up | *Me levanto a las siete.* |
| **despertarse** | me despierto | to wake up | *Me despierto a las seis y media.* |
| **ducharse** | me ducho | to shower | *Me ducho por la mañana.* |
| **vestirse** | me visto | to get dressed | *Me visto rápidamente.* |
| **desayunar** | desayuno | to have breakfast | *Desayuno café con tostada.* |
| **almorzar** | almuerzo | to have lunch | *Almuerzo a la una.* |
| **cenar** | ceno | to have dinner | *Ceno a las ocho.* |
| **trabajar** | trabajo | to work | *Trabajo hasta las cinco.* |
| **estudiar** | estudio | to study | *Estudio español por la noche.* |
| **acostarse** | me acuesto | to go to bed | *Me acuesto a las once.* |
| **dormirse** | me duermo | to fall asleep | *Me duermo rápido.* |

## Time Expressions

### Telling time

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| **Es la una.** | It's 1:00. |
| **Son las dos.** | It's 2:00. |
| **Son las tres y media.** | It's 3:30. |
| **Son las cuatro y cuarto.** | It's 4:15. |
| **A las siete.** | At 7:00. |
| **¿Qué hora es?** | What time is it? |

### Parts of the day

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| **por la mañana** | in the morning |
| **por la tarde** | in the afternoon |
| **por la noche** | at night |
| **de día** | by day |
| **de noche** | by night |

### Day structure

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| **temprano** | early |
| **tarde** | late (adverb) |
| **antes de** | before |
| **después de** | after |
| **durante el día** | during the day |
| **todos los días** | every day |

## Conversation Scenarios

### Scenario A — Morning routine

> **A:** ¿A qué hora te levantas?
> (What time do you get up?)
>
> **B:** Me levanto a las siete, pero me despierto a las seis y media.
> (I get up at 7, but I wake up at 6:30.)
>
> **A:** ¿Qué haces después?
> (What do you do after?)
>
> **B:** Me ducho, me visto y desayuno café con tostada.
> (I shower, get dressed, and have coffee with toast for breakfast.)
>
> **A:** ¿A qué hora sales de casa?
> (What time do you leave the house?)
>
> **B:** Salgo a las ocho y media. Trabajo hasta las cinco.
> (I leave at 8:30. I work until 5.)

### Scenario B — Evening routine

> **A:** ¿Qué haces por la noche?
> (What do you do at night?)
>
> **B:** Ceno a las ocho, estudio español una hora y me acuesto a las once.
> (I have dinner at 8, study Spanish for an hour, and go to bed at 11.)
>
> **A:** ¿Te duermes rápido?
> (Do you fall asleep quickly?)
>
> **B:** Sí, me duermo en cinco minutos.
> (Yes, I fall asleep in 5 minutes.)

### Scenario C — Workday summary

> *Me despierto a las 6:30, me levanto a las 7, y me ducho. Después, me visto, desayuno y salgo de casa a las 8:30. Trabajo desde las 9 hasta las 5. Por la tarde, estudio español una hora. Ceno a las 8 y me acuesto a las 11.*

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Fill in the reflexive pronoun

1. ___ levanto a las siete. (yo)
2. ___ duchas por la mañana. (tú)
3. ___ viste rápidamente. (él)
4. ___ acostamos a las once. (nosotros)
5. ___ acuestan temprano. (ellos)

2. Te
3. Se
4. Nos

## Role Play: Daily Routine (10 minutes)

### Scenario A — Interview

- **Your role:** Interviewer
- **My role:** Interviewee
- **Situation:** Ask about my daily routine — wake-up, work, meals, hobbies, bedtime

### Scenario B — Compare routines

- **Your role:** Interviewee
- **My role:** Interviewer
- **Situation:** Compare your routine with mine. Note similarities and differences (e.g., "Yo me levanto a las 7, pero tú te levantas a las 9")

|:---|:---|

## Pronunciation Guide

### Reflexive pronouns in fast speech

In connected speech, reflexive pronouns often blend with the following verb:

| Formal | Natural speech |
|:---|:---|
| *Me levanto* | [me leˈβanto] |
| *Te duchas* | [te ˈdutʃas] |
| *Se viste* | [se ˈβiste] |
| *Nos acostamos* | [nos akosˈtamos] |

### Key consonant sounds for Korean speakers

| Spanish sound | Example | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **r** (initial) | rápido | rolled / tapped r |
| **rr** | perro | strong roll |
| **ñ** | mañana | like "ny" in canyon |
| **j** | jugar | like a strong "h" |
| **h** | hablar | silent |
| **v / b** | vestido | pronounced the same |
