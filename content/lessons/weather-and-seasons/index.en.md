# Weather and Seasons (El Clima y las Estaciones)



## Lesson Overview

This lesson teaches how to describe weather conditions and the four seasons in Spanish. It introduces three distinct grammatical patterns that learners often confuse: **hacer** for general weather, **estar** for state + adjective, and dedicated verbs (**llover**, **nevar**) for specific phenomena.

**Goal:** Talk about today's weather, describe seasonal patterns, and make simple predictions.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Use **hacer** + weather noun for general conditions
2. Use **estar** + adjective for state descriptions
3. Conjugate **llover** (to rain) and **nevar** (to snow)
4. Name and describe the four seasons
5. Talk about temperature (cold, hot, mild)
6. Make simple weather predictions

## Grammar Pattern 1: Hacer + Weather (Most Common)

**Hacer** is used with impersonal weather expressions — there's no specific "subject" of the weather.

```
Hace + [weather condition]
```

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| **Hace calor** | It's hot |
| **Hace frío** | It's cold |
| **Hace sol** | It's sunny |
| **Hace viento** | It's windy |
| **Hace buen tiempo** | The weather is nice |
| **Hace mal tiempo** | The weather is bad |
| **Hacen las tres** | It's 3 o'clock (time expression) |

> **Korean speaker tip:** "It is hot" in Spanish is **Hace calor** — literally "It makes hot." Don't say *"Es caliente"* (which means "it is hot to the touch," e.g., for soup).

---

## Grammar Pattern 2: Estar + Adjective

**Estar** is used for current state with adjectives that describe weather conditions.

```
Está + [adjective]
```

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| **Está soleado** | It's sunny |
| **Está nublado** | It's cloudy |
| **Está lloviendo** | It's raining (present continuous) |
| **Está nevando** | It's snowing (present continuous) |
| **Está frío** | It's cold (state) |
| **Está caluroso** | It's hot (state) |
| **Está húmedo** | It's humid |
| **Está seco** | It's dry |
| **Está tormentoso** | It's stormy |

> **Note:** Use **estar** + present participle for **right now**; use **hacer** for general conditions.
> *Está lloviendo ahora* (It's raining right now) vs *Hace buen tiempo en verano* (The weather is nice in summer).

---

## Grammar Pattern 3: Llover / Nevar (3rd-person only)

These two verbs are typically used in the **3rd person singular** (only "it" rains / snows). The yo form exists but is rare.

### Llover (to rain) — o → ue stem change

| Person | Conjugation |
|:---:|:---|
| (yo) | (lluevo — rare) |
| (tú) | (llueves — rare) |
| **él/ella** | **llueve** |
| (nosotros) | (llovemos — rare) |
| (vosotros) | (llovéis — rare) |
| **ellos/ellas** | **llueven** |

### Nevar (to snow) — e → ie stem change

| Person | Conjugation |
|:---:|:---|
| (yo) | (nieve — rare) |
| (tú) | (nueves — rare) |
| **él/ella** | **nieve** |
| (nosotros) | (nevamos — rare) |
| (vosotros) | (neváis — rare) |
| **ellos/ellas** | **nievan** |

> **Korean speaker tip:** You don't need to memorize all 6 persons of these verbs. Focus on **llueve** (it rains) and **nieve** (it snows). The plural form is for emphatic effect: *Hoy llueven gotas grandes* (Today, big raindrops are falling).

## Seasonal Patterns

### Spring (la primavera)

> *En primavera **hace** buen tiempo. Las flores **florecen**.*
> (In spring, the weather is nice. Flowers bloom.)

| Common spring weather | Spanish |
|:---|:---|
| Sunny, mild | *Hace sol y está templado.* |
| Rain showers | *Llueve a veces.* |
| Windy | *Hace viento.* |

### Summer (el verano)

> *En verano **hace** mucho calor. La gente va a la playa.*
> (In summer, it's very hot. People go to the beach.)

| Common summer weather | Spanish |
|:---|:---|
| Very hot | *Hace mucho calor.* |
| Sunny | *Hace sol.* |
| Thunderstorms | *Hay tormentas.* |

### Autumn (el otoño)

> *En otoño las hojas **cambian** de color. **Hace** fresco por la mañana.*
> (In autumn, leaves change color. It's cool in the morning.)

| Common autumn weather | Spanish |
|:---|:---|
| Cool mornings | *Por la mañana hace fresco.* |
| Rain | *Llueve bastante.* |
| Wind | *Hace mucho viento.* |

### Winter (el invierno)

> *En invierno **hace** frío y a veces **nieve**.*
> (In winter, it's cold and sometimes it snows.)

| Common winter weather | Spanish |
|:---|:---|
| Cold | *Hace mucho frío.* |
| Snow | *Nieve en las montañas.* |
| Fog | *Hay niebla.* |

## Asking About the Weather

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| *¿Qué tiempo hace hoy?* | What's the weather like today? |
| *¿Qué tiempo hace?* | How's the weather? |
| *¿Hace calor?* | Is it hot? |
| *¿Está lloviendo?* | Is it raining? |
| *¿Va a llover mañana?* | Is it going to rain tomorrow? |
| *¿Cuál es la temperatura?* | What's the temperature? |

### Sample dialogue

> **A:** ¿Qué tiempo hace hoy?
> (What's the weather like today?)
>
> **B:** Hace frío y está nublado. Creo que va a llover por la tarde.
> (It's cold and cloudy. I think it's going to rain this afternoon.)
>
> **A:** ¿Y mañana?
> (And tomorrow?)
>
> **B:** Mañana hace sol, pero hace mucho viento.
> (Tomorrow it's sunny, but it's very windy.)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Scenario A — Morning chat

> **A:** ¿Qué tiempo hace hoy?
> (How's the weather today?)
>
> **B:** Hace frío y está nublado. Creo que va a nevar.
> (It's cold and cloudy. I think it's going to snow.)
>
> **A:** ¡Qué bien! Me gusta la nieve.
> (Great! I like the snow.)

### Scenario B — Planning the weekend

> **A:** ¿Qué tiempo va a hacer el fin de semana?
> (What's the weather going to be this weekend?)
>
> **B:** El sábado hace sol, pero el domingo llueve.
> (Saturday it's sunny, but Sunday it rains.)
>
> **A:** Entonces vamos a la playa el sábado.
> (Then let's go to the beach on Saturday.)

### Scenario C — Travel planning

> **A:** Voy a Madrid en julio. ¿Qué tiempo hace?
> (I'm going to Madrid in July. What's the weather?)
>
> **B:** ¡En julio hace muchísimo calor! Lleva ropa ligera y agua.
> (In July it's super hot! Bring light clothing and water.)
