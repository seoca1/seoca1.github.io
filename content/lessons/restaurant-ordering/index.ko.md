## 🇺🇸 Full English Content

# Restaurant Ordering (Pedir Comida)



## Lesson Overview

This lesson teaches how to navigate a restaurant in Spanish — from seating to ordering, dealing with dietary needs, and asking for the bill. It emphasizes the **gustar construction** (for expressing preferences) and the **quisiera** polite request form.

**Goal:** Confidently order food, ask about menu items, handle common requests (allergies, water, the check), and adapt to Spanish-speaking dining customs.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Use **quisiera** to make polite requests
2. Apply **gustar** to express menu preferences
3. Order food and drinks with correct articles and quantities
4. Ask about ingredients and dietary restrictions
5. Request the check and handle payment
6. Understand Spanish and Argentinian meal-time differences

---

## Core Grammar 1: The Gustar Construction

**Gustar** means "to please" rather than "to like" — Spanish reverses the structure compared to English. Full breakdown in [[Gustar_Verb]].

```
indirect object pronoun + gusta/gustan + subject
```

### Quick examples

> *Me **gusta** la paella.* (I like paella.)
> *Nos **gustan** los mariscos.* (We like the seafood.)

### 6 indirect object pronouns

| Pronoun | Person |
|:---:|:---|
| me | 1st sg. |
| te | 2nd sg. |
| le | 3rd sg. |
| nos | 1st pl. |
| os | 2nd pl. |
| les | 3rd pl. |

### gusta vs. gustan

| Subject | Verb form |
|:---|:---|
| Singular noun / infinitive | **gusta** |
| Plural noun / multiple infinitives | **gustan** |

---

## Core Grammar 2: Quisiera (Polite Request)

**Quisiera** is the conditional form of **querer** (to want). It expresses polite desire — softer than "quiero" (I want).

| Form | Tone | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| *Quiero…* | direct | *Quiero la paella.* |
| **Quisiera…** | polite ✅ | *Quisiera la paella, por favor.* |
| *Me gustaría…* | very polite | *Me gustaría ver el menú.* |

### Common phrases with quisiera

> ***Quisiera** una mesa para dos.* (I'd like a table for two.)
> ***Quisiera** pedir la cuenta.* (I'd like to ask for the check.)
> ***Quisiera** un café.* (I'd like a coffee.)

## Conversation Scenarios

### Scenario A — Arriving and seating

> **You:** Buenas tardes. ¿Tienen una mesa para dos?
> (Good afternoon. Do you have a table for two?)
>
> **Waiter:** Sí, por aquí, por favor. Aquí tiene la carta.
> (Yes, this way, please. Here's the menu.)
>
> **You:** Gracias. ¿Cuál es el especial de hoy?
> (Thank you. What's today's special?)

### Scenario B — Ordering

> **Waiter:** ¿Qué van a tomar?
> (What will you have?)
>
> **You:** Para mí, la paella, por favor.
> (For me, the paella, please.)
>
> **You:** Y para beber, agua con gas.
> (And to drink, sparkling water.)
>
> **Waiter:** ¿Quieren algo de postre?
> (Do you want any dessert?)
>
> **You:** Sí, el flan, por favor.
> (Yes, flan, please.)

### Scenario C — Dietary restrictions

> **You:** Perdón, soy alérgico a los frutos secos. ¿Este plato tiene almendras?
> (Excuse me, I'm allergic to nuts. Does this dish have almonds?)
>
> **Waiter:** No, no tiene almendras. Pero tiene cacahuetes.
> (No, it doesn't have almonds. But it has peanuts.)
>
> **You:** Entonces prefiero otro plato. ¿Qué recomienda sin gluten?
> (Then I'd prefer another dish. What do you recommend without gluten?)

### Scenario D — Getting the check

> **You:** Camarero, ¿puede traer la cuenta, por favor?
> (Waiter, can you bring the check, please?)
>
> **Waiter:** Sí, ahora mismo. ¿Está incluida la propina?
> (Yes, right away. Is the tip included?)
>
> **You:** No, gracias. La cuenta sin la propina, por favor.
> (No, thanks. The check without the tip, please.)

## Cultural Note (Nota Cultural)

### Spanish meal times

| Meal | Spain | Argentina | Korea |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Desayuno** (breakfast) | 7–9 AM (light) | 7–10 AM (café con medialunas) | 7–9 AM |
| **Almuerzo** (lunch) | 14:00–16:00 (main) | 12:30–14:30 (main) | 12:00–13:00 |
| **Merienda** (tea) | 17:00–19:00 (snack) | 17:00–19:00 (mate) | (rare) |
| **Cena** (dinner) | 21:00–23:00 (lighter) | 21:00–23:00 (main) | 18:00–20:00 |

> **Korean learner note:** Spanish dinner at 9–11 PM feels late. Plan accordingly — eat a snack around 18:00 if needed.

### Tipping customs

| Country | Standard tip |
|:---|:---|
| Spain | 5–10% (often included as "service") |
| Argentina | 10% (usually not included) |
| Mexico | 10–15% |
| Korea | Not customary |

### Common dining etiquette

1. **Greet on entering:** *Buenas tardes.* before being seated.
2. **Wait to be seated:** In Spain, you don't seat yourself.
3. **Keep both hands visible:** Unlike some cultures, hands on the table is fine.
4. **Pinch fingers to summon the waiter** (common in Spain).
5. **"Buen provecho"** means "Enjoy your meal" — say it to companions.
