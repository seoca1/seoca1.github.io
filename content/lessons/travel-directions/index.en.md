# Travel & Directions (Viajes y Direcciones)



## Lesson Overview

This lesson teaches how to ask for and give directions in Spanish-speaking countries. It covers **prepositions** (the grammatical foundation), **transport vocabulary**, **location/direction expressions**, and the **formal imperative (usted command form)** — essential for polite interaction with strangers.

**Goal:** Confidently navigate a Spanish-speaking city by asking for directions, understanding responses, and giving directions to others.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Ask for and understand directions in Spanish
2. Use public transportation vocabulary (bus, metro, taxi, train)
3. Describe locations using prepositions
4. Give directions using formal commands (Ud.)
5. Read common urban signage (calle, plaza, esquina)

---

## Core Grammar 1: Essential Prepositions

Quick recap of the most-used prepositions. Full breakdown in [Prepositions Spanish](/concepts/spanish-prepositions/).

| Spanish | English | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **a** | to / at | *Voy **a** Madrid.* |
| **de** | from / of | *Soy **de** Corea.* |
| **en** | in / on / by | *Estoy **en** la plaza.* |
| **hasta** | until / up to | ***Hasta** la esquina.* |
| **desde** | from (a starting point) | ***Desde** aquí, diez minutos.* |
| **entre** | between | ***Entre** la plaza y el museo.* |

### Contractions

| Article | a + el | de + el |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Result | **al** | **del** |

> *Voy **al** museo.* (I'm going **to the** museum.)
> *Vengo **del** centro.* (I'm coming **from** downtown.)

## Core Grammar 2: Formal Commands (Imperativo — Usted)

When giving directions to strangers, use the **formal (Ud.) command form**. The endings depend on the verb's group.

### -AR verbs: end in -e

| Infinitive | Ud. command | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| seguir (to follow) | **siga** | *Siga esta calle.* |
| girar (to turn) | **gire** | *Gire a la derecha.* |
| caminar (to walk) | **camine** | *Camine recto.* |
| cruzar (to cross) | **cruce** | *Cruce el puente.* |
| tomar (to take) | **tome** | *Tome el metro.* |

### -ER / -IR verbs: end in -a

| Infinitive | Ud. command | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| volver (to return) | **vuelva** | *Vuelva a la plaza.* |
| ir (to go) | **vaya** | *Vaya todo recto.* |
| tener (to have) | **tenga** | *Tenga cuidado.* |

### Common irregular commands

| Spanish | English | Ud. command |
|:---|:---|:---|
| ir | to go | **vaya** |
| hacer | to do/make | **haga** |
| poner | to put | **ponga** |
| tener | to have | **tenga** |
| salir | to leave | **salga** |
| venir | to come | **venga** |
| decir | to say | **diga** |

> **Korean speaker tip:** The final `-e` or `-a` sound is soft, similar to Korean polite endings ("-세요"). It's less abrupt than English imperative forms.

## Asking for Directions

### Essential questions

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| *¿Cómo llego a…?* | How do I get to…? |
| *¿Cómo puedo ir a…?* | How can I get to…? |
| *¿Dónde está…?* | Where is…? |
| *¿Está lejos de aquí?* | Is it far from here? |
| *¿Está cerca?* | Is it close? |
| *¿Cuánto tiempo se tarda?* | How long does it take? |
| *¿Puedo ir a pie?* | Can I walk there? |
| *¿Hay un autobús?* | Is there a bus? |

### Follow-up clarifications

| Spanish | English |
|:---|:---|
| *¿Perdón?* | Excuse me? (didn't hear) |
| *¿Puede repetir?* | Can you repeat? |
| *¿Puede hablar más despacio?* | Can you speak more slowly? |
| *No entiendo.* | I don't understand. |

## Conversation Scenarios

### Scenario A — Asking for the museum

> **You:** Perdón, ¿cómo llego al museo?
> (Excuse me, how do I get to the museum?)
>
> **Stranger:** Está un poco lejos. Vaya todo recto dos cuadras, luego gire a la derecha. El museo está a la izquierda, en frente del banco.
> (It's a bit far. Go straight for two blocks, then turn right. The museum is on the left, in front of the bank.)
>
> **You:** ¿Cuánto tiempo se tarda?
> (How long does it take?)
>
> **Stranger:** Unos quince minutos a pie, o cinco en autobús.
> (About 15 minutes walking, or 5 by bus.)
>
> **You:** Muchas gracias.
> (Thank you very much.)

### Scenario B — At the metro station

> **You:** ¿Este tren va al centro?
> (Does this train go downtown?)
>
> **Agent:** Sí, pero tiene que hacer transbordo en Sol.
> (Yes, but you have to transfer at Sol.)
>
> **You:** ¿En qué línea?
> (On which line?)
>
> **Agent:** Línea 3. Salga en la tercera parada.
> (Line 3. Get off at the third stop.)
